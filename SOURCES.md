# Sources & References

## Academic & Scholarly Works

### Definitive Study
- **Lysaght, P.** (1986). *The Banshee: The Irish Death Messenger*. Roberts Rinehart Publishers. ISBN: 978-0906602676
  - **The** definitive academic study of banshee tradition
  - Comprehensive analysis of folklore, regional variations, and historical accounts
  - Essential reading for serious banshee research

### Irish Folklore Collections
- Ó Súilleabháin, S. (1942). *A Handbook of Irish Folklore*. Educational Company of Ireland.
  - Systematic folklore collection methodology
  - Includes banshee accounts

- Danaher, K. (1972). *The Year in Ireland*. Mercier Press.
  - Irish customs and traditions including death customs

### Celtic Mythology
- MacKillop, J. (1998). *Dictionary of Celtic Mythology*. Oxford University Press. ISBN: 978-0198609674
  - Comprehensive entry on banshee and related beings

- Rees, A. & Rees, B. (1961). *Celtic Heritage: Ancient Tradition in Ireland and Wales*. Thames & Hudson.
  - Cultural context for Irish death omens

## Classic Folklore Collections

### W.B. Yeats
- Yeats, W.B. (1888). *Fairy and Folk Tales of the Irish Peasantry*. Various editions.
  - Classic collection including banshee lore
  - Literary treatment of Irish folklore

- Yeats, W.B. (1892). *Irish Fairy Tales*. T. Fisher Unwin.
  - Additional fairy and banshee stories

### Lady Gregory
- Gregory, Lady Augusta (1920). *Visions and Beliefs in the West of Ireland*. G.P. Putnam's Sons.
  - First-hand folklore collection from Western Ireland
  - Direct accounts from Irish people
  - Includes banshee testimonies

### Other Classic Collections
- Wilde, Lady Jane (1888). *Ancient Legends, Mystic Charms, and Superstitions of Ireland*. Ward and Downey.
  - Extensive Irish folklore including banshee traditions

- Curtin, J. (1890). *Myths and Folk-Lore of Ireland*. Little, Brown, and Company.
  - Irish mythological traditions

- Croker, T.C. (1825-1828). *Fairy Legends and Traditions of the South of Ireland*. John Murray.
  - Early collection of Irish fairy folklore

## Death & Funeral Customs

### Keening Tradition
- Ó Madagáin, B. (1985). "Functions of Irish Song in the Nineteenth Century." *Béaloideas*, 53, 130-216.
  - Analysis of keening and funeral songs

- Lysaght, P. (1997). "Caoineadh os Cionn Coirp: The Semantic Structure of Irish Keen." In *Oral Tradition*, 12(1).
  - Academic analysis of keening tradition

- Bourke, A. (1993). "More in Anger than in Sorrow: Irish Women's Lament Poetry." In *Feminist Messages: Coding in Women's Folk Culture*. University of Illinois Press.
  - Keening as women's cultural practice

## Research Notes

### Primary Sources
- First-hand accounts from Irish Folklore Commission archives
- Direct testimony from Irish people (Gregory, etc.)
- Family oral traditions

### Secondary Sources
- Academic analysis (Lysaght is gold standard)
- Comparative folklore studies
- Cultural and anthropological contexts

### Living Tradition
- The banshee is not merely historical
- Ongoing reports from Irish families
- Treat contemporary accounts with respect for cultural context
